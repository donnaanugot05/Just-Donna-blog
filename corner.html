Born in the vibrant city of Mati, Davao Oriental, on a warm March day, I, Donna Jean Anugot, began a life painted with both joy and trials. Even as a child, a deep well of kindness resided within me, a trait that was tested early when I intervened in a market robbery. That moment etched into my soul the importance of compassion, a guiding star in my journey. Growing up in Madang was no gentle affair. The harshness of our community, the envy and resentment directed towards my government-working parents, cast long shadows over my childhood. Though they tried to shield me, the weight of their struggles became my own, a constant battle against negativity. I fought to stay true to myself, to not become the very thing I despised, but the pressure chipped away at my gentleness, leaving me disillusioned. School, initially a breeze, became a crucible in high school, especially during the pandemic. I learned to balance ambition with reality, striving for personal best rather than mere superiority. My friendships were forged in the pages of books and the scenes of movies, my social skills honed in journalism and Girl Scouting. Teenage years demanded resilience. Academic duties, family responsibilities, and financial hardships converged, culminating in a local pageant to fund my education. That experience, won with sheer determination and the unwavering support of my brother, taught me the power of hard work and the value of family. College presented new financial challenges, met with tutoring, pageantry, and scholarships. I learned the true cost of money, the effort required to earn and manage it. My drive to excel, however, led me down a dark path of neglect, sacrificing my well-being for academic achievement. The realization that I had lost myself was a stark awakening. Now, at 19, I reflect with a mix of pride and longing. I am stronger, more self-aware, but I miss the simplicity, the boundless imagination of my younger self. If I could, I would beg her forgiveness for the damage I've done. I grapple with the fear of an early end, yet I hold onto hope. I dream of providing for my family, building a loving relationship, exploring the world, and achieving the success I deserve. My future, a canvas of endless possibilities, is painted with the lessons of resilience, empathy, and self-awareness. Though I may have strayed from the path of my younger self, I carry her spirit within me, a reminder of the kindness and strength that define me.
